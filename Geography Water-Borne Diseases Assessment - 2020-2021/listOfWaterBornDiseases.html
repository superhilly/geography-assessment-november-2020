List of water-borne diseases

There are many different water related diseases, below are the majority accompanied by a description and an image of the disease. (in alphebetical order)

DISCLAIMER:Some of the images below may cause uncomfort to some readers.


Anaemia

A condition where you lack enough healthy red blood cells. Symptoms might include fatigue, pale or yellow skin, chest pain, irregular heartbeats, etc. However, some people might not show any symptoms.

Arsenicosis

A chronic illness resulting from drinking water with high levels of arsenic over a long period of time.Symptoms include red or swollen skin, nausea, muscle cramps and abnormal heart rhythm.

Ascariasis

An infection of the small intestine caused by Ascaris lumbricoides, which is a species of roundworm.Symptoms include abdominal pain, vomiting, weight loss and fatigue.

Botulism

A disease caused by a toxin produced by the bacterium Clostridium botulinum. The disease begins with weakness, blurred vision, feeling tired, and trouble speaking.

Campylobacteriosis

An infection by the Campylobacter bacteria. It is among the most common bacterial infections of humans, often a foodborne illness.

Cholera

An infectious disease that causes severe watery diarrhea and dehydration. Caused by eating food or drinking water contaminated with the bacteria Vibrio Cholerae.

Cryptosporiodiosis

An illness caused by a parasite that lives in soil, food and water. People get infected by swallowing food/drinks or swimming in areas that are contaminated by the parasite.

Cyanobacterial toxins

Deadly toxins made by cyanobacteria that can affect the liver. Symptoms include headache, nausea, abdominal pain, muscle aches and mouth ulcers.

Dengue

Dengue is a mosquito-borne tropical disease caused by the dengue virus.

Diarrhoea

Diarrhoea is when your bowel movements become loose or watery.

Dracunculiasis

Dracunculiasis is a parasitic infection caused by the Guinea worm.

Fluorosis

Fluorosis is a cosmetic condition that affects the teeth.

Giardiasis

Giardiasis is a parasitic disease caused by Giardia duodenalis.

Hepatitis

an inflammatory condition of the liver

Hookworm infection

an infection of the intestines that can cause an itchy rash, respiratory and gastrointestinal problems, and eventually iron deficiency anemia due to ongoing loss of blood

Japanese encephalitis

a disease spread through mosquito bites

Lead poisoning

Acute poisoning caused by absorption of lead into the body, symptoms include kidney damage, risk of high blood pressure etc

Legionellosis

A form of atypical pneumonia caused by any type of Legionellosis bacteria, symptoms include coughing, short breath, diarrhea

Leptospirosis

A blood infection caused by the bacteria Leptospira, symptoms include muscle pains and fevers

Lymphatic filariasis

Considered a neglected tropical disease, a parasitic disease caused by thread-like worms, symptoms include limb swelling, fever, skin exfoliation

Malaria

A life threatening disease, transmitted through a bite of an infected mosquito, releasing the parasite into your bloodstream, symptoms include high temperature, feeling hot and shivery and vomiting.

Malnutrition

Caused by having an inadequate diet or a problem absorbing nutrients from food. Symptoms include feeling tired all the time, getting ill often and taking a long time to recover.

Methemoglobinemia

Is a condition of elevated methemoglobin in the blood, symptoms include headaches, nausea etc.

Onchocerciasis

A disease that affects skin and eyes, caused by the worm Onchocera volvulus (a parasite), symptoms include skin rashes, itchy eyes, loss of skin elasticity.

Polio

Polio, also known as poliomyelitis, is a disease that can disable and threaten the infected caused by the poliovirus. The virus is contagious (spreads from person to person) and can infect a personâ€™s spinal cord, causing paralysis (disablility in movement in parts of the body).

Ringworm or Tinea

Ringworm, also known as dermatophytosis or tinea, is a fungal infection of the skin. Tinea is a fungus, not a worm.

Scabies

Scabies is caused by a mite known as the Sarcoptes scabiei and is a skin infestation. If untreated, the microscopic mites can live on the skin for long periods of time (months).

Schistosomiasis

Schistosomiasis is an acute and chronic disease caused by parasitic worms. According to WHO, as of 2018 estimates show that at least 290.8 million people required preventive treatment for schistosomiasis, out of which more than 97.2 million people were reported to have been treated of the disease.

Trachoma

Trachoma is an infectious disease caused by bacterium Chlamydia trachomatis. It is a public health problem in 44 countries around the world, and is the cause of blindness or visual impairment of approximately 1.9 million people.

Trichuriasis

Trichuriasis is an infection by the parasitic worm Trichuris trichiura (also known as the whipworm). The parasite usually takes up residence in the terminal ileum (the end of the small intestine) and cecum (beginning of the large intestine).

Typhoid

Typhoid is a bacterial infection that can lead to high fever, diarrhea, and vomiting and can be fatal for people with a lack of improved water. It is caused by a bacteria called Salmonella typhi.
